PSYCHO METHODOLOGY

The Core Strategy

Traditional job hunting (mass-applying) is dead. Great startups hire from relationships and out-of-band proof of value. Your goal: obsess over 3-5 high-conviction targets and force your way in by solving their problems before they even interview you.

1. Spotting Signal

Use the Venture Intel dashboard feeds:

  • Ramp Reports: Spot categories with growing spend. Target the fastest-growing vendors (they need headcount).
  • Harmonic Hot 25: Identify startups with massive headcount velocity spikes. They are actively scale-hiring.
  • YC Directory: Target newly funded early-stage batches (W26/S25). They have fresh capital and need builders.

2. Digital Reconnaissance

Find the founders and core team members on Twitter/X and get their emails.

Tip: Search the company's official Twitter account, and check its followers list. The founders and early engineers almost always follow it. Target team members with fewer followers; they are far more likely to read and reply.

3. Open Source Jackpot

If their codebase is open source: You hit the jackpot. Stop reading and start contributing! Fix bugs, optimize files, improve documentation. Your name will appear directly in their repository before you pitch them.

If they are closed-source or you are in a non-technical role: observe their landing page, docs, or community channels. Write down 5 friction points and brainstorm clear, actionable solutions.

4. Authentically Interact

Engage with the founders' tweets and posts. Suggest ideas, ask creative questions, and share helpful resources. Avoid sycophant/jerk vibes. Build a positive digital relationship so they recognize your name when you reach out.

5. The Value Pitch

Create a detailed project/doc (Notion/Obsidian) specifically for them. Show, don't tell:

  • Engineers: Code a working prototype of a requested feature, write a comprehensive test suite, or draft a refactor plan.
  • Growth/Ops: A 30-day community launch blueprint, copy rewrites, or a breakdown of marketing channels.
Pitch it via direct email or Cold DM. "Hey [Name], built this for you guys to solve [Problem]. Hope it helps!"
